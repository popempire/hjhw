### Young Henry
is derived from the ballad *Henry Lee* or *Love Henry*, an Appalachian descendant
of the Scottish ballad *Young Hunting*
- Henry Lee
  - Dick Justice
  - Dialogue with Henry
  - Dialogue with the bird
  - poetic justice
- Young Hunting
  - Roud 47, Child 68
  - Nancy Kerr & James Fagan
  - the rest of the story
    - disposal of the body
    - king's party & investigation
    - return of the talking bird
    - retrieval of the body & execution
  - what has been removed?
    - superstition-based evidence
    - depiction of a woman being burned at the stake
    - obvious class differences

### Black Dog Blues
is lyrically inspired by Roscoe Holcomb's song *Graveyard Blues* and
anj's album *The Year of the Raccoon*
  - Graveyard Blues
  - The Year of the Raccoon "poem for a tree"

### Preachin' the Devil
is inspired by the Louvin Brothers' *Satan Is Real*, seminal recordings in rock
n roll history by Bessie Smith, Bo Diddley, and the Rolling Stones, and musings
on the nature of the Devil
  - Satan Is Real
    - the album cover
    - the reality of Satan for Charlie & Ira Louvin
  - a dark sexual power?
    - Bessie Smith, Preachin' the Blues
    - Who Do You Love?
    - Sympathy for the Devil
  - musings
    - god & the devil
    - son house "if you use the words right"
    - I-75 "hell is real"
    - paradise lost

### Lagan Dream
is an adaptation of the Irish air known in English as *My Lagan Love*, first
(and beautifully) translated by Joseph Campbell, with infusions of verse from other sources
  - (keep it short)

### God's Grandeur
is a sonnet by Gerard Manley Hopkins set to music derived from the blues standard
*Sittin' On Top of the World*
  - nature is suffused with the divine
  - the state of sin is estrangement from nature
  - masculine/feminine aspects of the divine
  - hopkins on prosody
    - sprung rhythm
    - setting the text to music

### Dyin' Silver Dagger Blues
is an amalgamation of the folk song *Silver Dagger*, the ballad *Frankie and Johnny*,
and Blind Willie McTell's *Dying Crapshooter's Blues* (itself an amalgamation 
of numerous tunes with deep roots in English & Irish folk songs)
  - Silver Dagger
    - Joan Baez
    - recalls tradition of warning songs for young women
    - trauma/neurosis of one generation has dire consquences for the next
  - Frankie & Johnny
    - Jimmie Rodgers
    - Frankie Baker
    - Frankie Silver
  - Dying Crapshooter's Blues

### Rock Me
is a composition by Thomas Dorsey, first recorded by Sister Rosetta Tharpe,
that sits near the fountainhead of soul and rock n roll music
  - sister rosetta tharpe "for the rock n roll spiritual singing"
    - 1938 version
    - lucky millinder version(s)
  - many versions, including Lead Belly and Blind Willie McTell
  - god & the devil (rock n roll)
  - calls to mind brooding mother of God's Grandeur

### The Living End
is a composition by Jim & William Reid (The Jesus and Mary Chain)
  - (keep it short)
  - motorcycle imagery has many referents, Ghost Rider, Suicide
  - both solipsistic nihilism and succint moral
  - formally elegant blues

### Mother Earth
is a composition by Peter Chatmon (Memphis Slim)
  - 1951 version
  - 1970 version
  - infinite variety of possible verses!
  - fascination with death in folk/pop song
  - a naturalistic understanding of death
    - If I Should Fall From Grace With God "let me go down in the mud"
    - anj, traveler "take it all back to the earth"
