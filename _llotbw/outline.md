### Young Henry
is derived from the ballad *Henry Lee* or *Love Henry*, an Appalachian descendant
of the Scottish ballad *Young Hunting*
- Young Hunting
- Henry Lee

### Black Dog Blues
is inspired by Roscoe Holcomb's song *Graveyard Blues*, 
anj's album *The Year of the Raccoon*,
and The Pogues' song *If I Should Fall From Grace With God*
  - Graveyard Blues
  - a naturalistic understanding of death
  - If I Should Fall From Grace With God
  - The Year of the Raccoon

### Preachin' the Devil
is inspired by the Louvin Brothers' *Satan Is Real*, seminal recordings in rock
n roll history by Bessie Smith, Bo Diddley, and the Rolling Stones, and musings
on the nature of the Devil
  - Satan Is Real
  - Bessie Smith, Preachin' the Devil
  - Who Do You Love?
  - Sympathy for the Devil
  - musings
    - god & the devil
    - son house "if you use the words right"
    - I-75 "hell is real"
    - paradise lost

### Lagan Dream
is an adaptation of the Irish air known in English as *My Lagan Love*, first
(and beautifully) translated by Joseph Campbell, with infusions of verse from other sources
  - (keep it short)

### God's Grandeur
is a sonnet by Gerard Manley Hopkins set to music derived from the blues standard
*Sittin' On Top of the World*
  - nature is suffused with the divine
  - the state of sin is estrangement from nature
  - masculine/feminine aspects of the divine

### Dyin' Silver Dagger Blues
is an amalgamation of the folk song *Silver Dagger*, the ballad *Frankie and Johnny*,
and Blind Willie McTell's *Dying Crapshooter's Blues* (itself an amalgamation 
of numerous tunes with deep roots in English & Irish folk songs)
  - Frankie & Johnny
    - Frankie Baker
    - Frankie Silver
  - Silver Dagger

is a composition by Thomas Dorsey, first recorded by Sister Rosetta Tharpe,
that sits near the fountainhead of soul and rock n roll music
